18 Artists Who Are Redefining Design In The Digital Age.

Dan LuVisi

Dan LuVisi is not only a digital artist specializing in illustration focused on bizarre pop-culture parodies, but he’s also a writer. Based in Southern California, big companies have trusted his creative prowess. He’s worked with DC Comics, Lionsgate, Microsoft, Universal, Fox, Activision, and more.

Go Follow
Steven Harrington

Growing up with such supportive parents, Steven Harrington pursued his passion and love for painting and drawing. Although he has been to many places, he still considers his city Los Angeles a significant influence on his art. You’ll see many psychedelic pop styles in his portfolio, including hand-screened prints, sculptures, and large-scale installations.

Go Follow
Christophe Young

Based in Montreal, Christophe Young’s work revolves around fantasy-themed characters and science fiction. So if you love anime, you can surely relate to this digital artist and his beautiful artwork. Christophe Young is responsible for some of the DLC characters, namely The Plague, The Spirit, The Oni, The Legion, Jane, Yui, Jeff, and more.

Go Follow
Calob Castellon

Calob Castellon is a digital artist and photographer who transforms ordinary photos into surprisingly astonishing ones. He shows the raw photos and clips on his Instagram account and publishes the final ones with digital effects.

Go Follow
Joan Cornellà

Joan Cornellà is from Barcelona and is one of the most popular digital artists famous for his startling work of art. Joan Cornellà’s work is described as offensive and morbid. At first glance, you’ll notice the characters seemingly smiling and naturally cheerful. However, upon further inspection, you’ll see that there is a dark and sinister story behind the artwork.

Go Follow
Flore Maquin

If you’re into movies, then Flore Maquin is the digital artist to follow. The French graphic designer remakes movie posters and transforms them into more appealing visuals. Some examples are the posters from the movies Austin Powers, Django Unchained, Jaws, and more.

Go Follow
Eva Balloon

If you’re into movies, then Eva Balloon is the digital artist to follow. The French graphic designer remakes movie posters and transforms them into more appealing visuals. Some examples are the posters from the movies Austin Powers, Django Unchained, Jaws, and more.

Go Follow
Mike Meyers

If you’re into the outdoors, you’ll get inspired by Mike Meyers’ breathtaking photographs of natural landscapes and urban environments. His art showcases the grandeur of natural scenes or man-made buildings with stunning views.

Go Follow
Bosslogic

Kode Abdo, the digital artist behind Bosslogic, has been drawing since he was six years old. He started tinkering with superhero-themed art using Adobe Photoshop. Most of his artwork on Instagram are posters of the biggest movies in 2019.

Go Follow
Takashi Murakami

If you’re a fan of Japan and its pop culture scene, then you should definitely check out Takashi Murakami’s page. This digital artist blends Japanese traditional art and its pop culture scene. He is trained in Japanese art and his style banks on bold and striking imagery in 2D forms.

Go Follow
June Y. Cheng

June Cheng is the digital artist you must follow if you’re into beautiful portraits. She not only creates awe-inspiring female portraits, but her artwork has a soft and ethereal touch to them. Most of her paintings have a bright pallet with a sprinkle of magenta and cyan. Overall, the best word to describe her portfolio on Instagram is moving.

Go Follow
Gretel Lusky

Gretel Lusky is one of the digital artists who got inspired by 90s cartoons. Growing up, she developed a love for the fun and amusing comical characters and pursued her dream in comic illustrations.

Go Follow
Madiel Lopez

As colorful as Cuba’s culture, Magdiel Lopez’s artwork also dons the same vigor and vivid beauty. His childhood has a lot of influence on his art and style, which you can see on his Instagram account. Looking for greener pastures, Lopez migrated to the United States at fifteen years old while working as a private digital artist for 10 years.

Go Follow
John Aslarona

John Aslarona has always been fond of painting, the reason he enrolled in painting classes in high school. He then transitioned to digital painting in 2011 and motivated other up-and-coming artists through his beautiful art.

Go Follow
Niko Christian

Niko Christian is a young artist based in Las Vegas, Nevada. His style is more of imaginative concepts that explore the environment and human nature. He then transforms these works of art into a beautiful masterpiece with a somewhat psychedelic touch.

Go Follow
Shepard Fairey

Since Shepard Fairey was a kid, he was inclined into the skateboarding culture. He has then developed an interest in skateboarding, he started designing skateboards and t-shirts in 1984. He continued to pursue his art and got famous for Barack Obama’s poster in 2008. It was the iconic “Hope” poster that depicted entrepreneurial spirit and activism.

Go Follow
Christoph Niemann

Christoph Niemann is a visual storyteller, as written on his Instagram account. This digital artist moved to New York in 1997 and started as an editorial illustrator. His style banks on funny and unorthodox illustrations with a touch of hand-drawn paint and watercolor components.
